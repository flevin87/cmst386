Search Engines

Search engines use complex algorithms to crawl, index, and rank web pages based on relevance, quality, and user experience. By employing SEO best practices such as proper keyword use, quality content, site performance/speed, and proper link building, search engines are able to higher rank web pages and improve a site's visibility in search engine results.

The Importance of Key Words

Key Words are integral to SEO because they are a part of the relationship between the content one's website offers and the content that users search for. Due to the nature of search engines, smaller databases are responsible for storing keyword-based indexes - thus a developer should choose their keywords based on relevancy and where they wish to be ranked. The goal is to rank highly for keywords that you believe people are searching for when they want what your website provides.

Meta Tag Descriptions

A meta tag description is a brief summary of a website's content, and it appears below the title on a search engine results page. While this description doesn't affect rankings, it is important to choose an effective description, often using your keyword, to boost traffic and conversions - which will influence your rankings.

Four Elements of Creating Relevant Content Links

  1. Anchor Text: The clickable text in a hyperlink must be relevant to the page that you are linking to.
  2. Quality Links: Each link should direct the user to high-quality, dependable, and relevant resources.
  3. Relevancy: The linked content should always be closely related to the content on your own website. This helps search engines understand the relationship between separate pieces of content.
  4. Quantity: As a developer, one should not make their content to be spammy - try to balance this out by having a reasonable number of high-quality links.

Understanding Hierarchy

A well established hierarchy is a vital part of readability for users and SEO. It organizes your content logically based off of importance and relevance, which helps users and search engines understand the relationship between content and the structure of your page.
